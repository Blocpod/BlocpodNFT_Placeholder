01 · What already exists

The hard part is already built: a differentiated physical world.

Tequila Town Miami is publicly positioned as a 25,000+ square-foot immersive agave experience with 10+ rooms, blending art, architecture, music, ritual, storytelling, drinks, food, and the cultural energy of Miami. The proposal does not replace that experience. It makes the experience measurable, repeatable, ownable, and scalable.

01

Physical storytelling

The venue already gives guests a journey through tequila’s origins, cultural intersections, immersive environments, food, drinks, and music.

02

Commercial infrastructure

Ticketing, private-event demand, brand activations, hospitality, and visitor flow create immediate surfaces for digital integration and revenue testing.

03

Scalable format potential

The room-based experience can become a documented operating system that supports permanent venues, touring installations, and licensed city deployments.

The opportunity is not to “add an app.”

It is to create a persistent digital relationship before, during, and after each guest visit—then use the Miami venue to prove the model, improve it with real behavior, and package it for expansion.

02 · Venue integration

One guest journey. Five moments of value creation.

The platform should connect the complete customer lifecycle instead of treating ticket purchase, venue attendance, and post-visit engagement as separate transactions.

Discover

Explore the venue, upcoming events, cultural stories, featured producers, and personalized reasons to visit.

Prepare

Buy or connect a ticket, complete a Taste Profile, select interests, and receive a personalized pre-arrival itinerary.

Experience

Check in, unlock rooms, scan moments, follow stories, complete tastings, and collect Passport stamps throughout the venue.

Remember

Receive a shareable visit recap, bottles tasted, favorite rooms, cultural discoveries, photos, badges, and recommendations.

Return

Unlock rewards, private events, new tastings, premium membership, partner experiences, and future city trails.

04 · The proposal

Build the minimum system that proves the entire thesis.

Phase one should focus on the smallest integrated product that generates measurable guest adoption, repeat behavior, first-party data, sponsor value, and a clear blueprint for scale.

Recommended Phase-One Product

A mobile-first web app or installable PWA that begins before arrival, guides guests through the live venue, and keeps the relationship active after departure.

Core product principle:
Guests should receive value in under two minutes. Staff should not need to explain the system. Every interaction should strengthen Tequila Town’s owned customer record.

09 · Operating model

Clear ownership prevents the platform from becoming a disconnected side project.

Tequila Town Team

Owns brand authority, venue operations, guest experience, cultural programming, partner relationships, product approvals, and commercial priorities.

Technology + Product Team

Owns discovery, architecture, UX/UI, engineering, integrations, analytics, security, deployment, product iteration, and technical documentation.

Joint Product Council

A small decision group that controls scope, resolves blockers, approves customer-facing changes, reviews KPIs, and protects strategic alignment.

Partner Activation Function

Packages sponsor inventory, defines measurement, coordinates creative assets, launches campaigns, and converts results into renewals and case studies.

Investment should follow discovery.

A final budget and delivery schedule should be issued only after venue walkthrough, systems inventory, ticketing review, data/privacy assessment, content audit, stakeholder interviews, and phase-one scope lock. This prevents false precision and protects both teams from avoidable change orders.

10 · Strategic guardrails

What this must not become.

Not a generic tequila database

The differentiator is the living Tequila Town experience, proprietary identity, and owned cultural formats—not an endless bottle catalog.

Not a feature-heavy launch

Phase one must prove a tight guest loop before adding social feeds, marketplaces, certification, awards, or large partner tooling.

Not dependent on one venue term

The architecture must support a permanent flagship, a seasonal installation, a touring format, or a hybrid model without rebuilding the core product.
